What even is wishdropland ? 

It's just the universe, however layered with an intricate guess as to why it works the way it does. Well, the guess itself is pretty simple, actually. We call it the wishdrops conjecture. Let's disarm it by saying it plainly:

_A sphere spinning evenly on infinite axes projects the universe._

There's a lot in there, sure, but it's still just one sentence – not bad. The intricacy is everything that happens afterwards.

To be clear, we intend the conjecture strictly, as in mathematically, even as our manner of speaking is for the most part an informal appeal to intuition. In other words, we acknowledge we're not very rigorous about this, and yeah, that exposes us to being completely incorrect anywhere along the way, and if that turns out to be the case, well, it'll still be a good therapy session, so thanks for that, sit back and enjoy the delusion :) regardless, we're gonna keep it real, in that our goal remains to build a bridge from the spinning sphere to the real and actual laws of physics.

There are broader implications than what is obviously physics, too. Things like the sensory apparatus, including why we see the colors and hear the notes that we do; the cultural significance ascribed to certain physical phenomena; even morality; plus who knows what else ! Actually, this is where we started with all of this; we were intrigued by the correspondences between light wavelengths and color, and between sound wavelengths and notes, thus compelled to back our way into some understanding of physics, in order to explain, or to corroborate, these "observations" about sensory spectra. Such things tend to be the third rail in physics, but look at it this way -- if we're guessing why the universe works the way it does, there must be _some_ accounting for the nature of our experience in it. As such, we see this material as fair game. Plus we started there, as mentioned, so in addition to attempting to fortify our suspicion that the connection is real, we just want to talk about it !

The thought that our universe is girded by a simple geometry is not a terribly new idea. There have been a number of similar expressions throughout history. Pythagoras, Plato's fundamental shapes, David Bohm's "implicate vs explicate" forms, Wheeler's "it from bit", Penrose's twistor theory, prob others... Kepler ? Did he do the nested spheres ? We'll have to go look these up. Anyway, the idea that all the complexity and vastness we see in our universe is backed by something very simple and mathish is not without precedent. For sure, we are following in a longstanding tradition.

Before we elaborate on the sphere, let's be up front about something. It does not live in the universe. The theatre of the heavens we call the vacuum of space ? Yeah, we do not find the sphere anywhere in it. We propose, very literally, that it's the other way around. 

_Space, including time, matter, and all we observe, lives in the sphere._

Every particle surface, every fluctuation, every arc traced out by light against the supposed vacuum of space, all of it is a part of the singular sphere, however projected. For emphasis, let's say this again, perhaps in a slightly different way, more condensed, even as we don't expect it to make sense quite yet, because this cannot be overstated, at least in the context of our conjecture:

The universe is a sphere. Literally.

This may be difficult to square with our intuition about spheres, which are normally simple and sorta round, so we have a lot of work to do, undoubtedly, but for now suffice it to say that the extra dimensions and the spinning motion are crucial in a perhaps non-obvious way to the cosmic transformation. We'll work on that. Now, given its primacy in our conjecture, let's maybe capitalize The Sphere from now on, or even refer to it as The Device, to disambiguate from the regular spheres we'll probably also need to talk about.

Oh, one other point of clarity before we get way into it – the ambient space in which you might find our magical Device is irrelevant to our discussion. The Device on its own encompasses all of existence as we understand it, even within the confines of our fantastic story that may be a delusion. We don't care about the space around the Device. Call it the great mystery. Who knows where the sphere lives ? It is the relations between parts of the sphere that matter. Everything has meaning only _in relation_ to whatever else.

Not to downplay it now, but even though it constitutes everything, The Device is just a sphere. This indeed has a very precise mathematical meaning. 

_A sphere is all points at a set distance from a center point._

This is a useful definition. It means that a point is _not_ on the sphere _unless_ it lives at precisely the same set distance from the center as everything else. This distance, for what it's worth, is called the radius. Note, of course, that unless a point is at the radius, it means nothing to us. The thing we care about is like a bubble, not a ball, but even calling it a bubble is generous because in truth it has zero thickness. not a small thickness, but none. All "nearby" points to any point on the sphere also live on the sphere, at the same exact radius.

What about the infinite dimensions ?





before this reveal we may have been thinking about the ordinary bubbles we're used to. two dimensional "curved sheets" that exist in a three dimensional space. actually, let's revisit our intuition about dimensions – zero dimensions is a point, one dimension is a line, two dimensions is a plane, and three dimensions is a volume, remember ? very loosely speaking, of course. a poetic way of thinking about a dimension is that it's:

a new direction that casts no shadow

consider a point not on the established dimensional frame. now position a "light source" such that the point casts no shadow on the frame. or, well, a perfect little zero dimensional dot. the line between the light source and the point aligns perfectly with a new dimension.

 and this process can be repeated beyond three times, even if it is difficult to envision. math doesn't care.

now let's think deeply about the dimensions of a sphere. recall that a sphere is all points at a set radius. that can mean different things depending on the dimensionality of the space it exists in.
