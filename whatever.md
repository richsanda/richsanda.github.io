What even is wishdropland ? 

It's just the universe, however layered with an intricate guess as to why it works the way it does. Well, the guess itself is pretty simple, actually. We call this the wishdrops conjecture. Let's disarm it by saying it plainly:

_A sphere spinning evenly on infinite axes projects the universe._

There's a lot in there, sure, but it's still just one sentence – not bad. The intricacy is the extrapolation.

We intend this conjecture in the strictest mathematical sense, even as our manner of speaking is for the most part an informal appeal to intuition. We acknowledge that we're not very rigorous about this, which, yeah, exposes us to being completely incorrect anywhere along the way, but if that turns out to be the case, well, it'll still be a good therapy session, so thanks for that, and, well, sit back and enjoy the delusion :) Either way we're gonna keep it real – our goal remains to build a bridge from this spinning sphere to the laws of physics. We'll get into some physics-heavy topics before we're done for sure, even if only poetically.

There are broader implications than what is obviously physics too. Things like the sensory apparatus, including why we see the colors and hear the notes that we do; cultural significance ascribed to physical phenomena; even morality; plus who knows what else ! That's where we started, actually. We were intrigued by the correspondences between light wavelengths and color, and between sound wavelengths and notes, thus compelled to back our way into some understanding of physics, in order to explain, or to at least corroborate, these "observations" about sensory spectra. These sorts of things tend to be the third rail in discussions about physics, but, well, if we're guessing why the universe works the way it does, there must be some account for the nature of our experience in it. We see this as fair game, plus we started there, so besides just fortifying our suspicion that the connection is real, we just want to talk about it !

The thought that our universe is undergirded by a simple geometry is not a terribly new idea. There have been a number of expressions throughout history that posit the same sort of thing. Pythagoras, Plato's fundamental shapes, David Bohm's "implicate vs explicate" forms, Wheeler's "it from bit", Penrose's twistor theory, prob others… Kepler ? Anyway, the idea that all the complexity and vastness we see in our universe is backed by something very simple and mathish is not without precedent. For sure, this is what we're doing too.

Before we elaborate on the sphere, we want to be clear about something very important up front. It does not live in the universe. The theatre of the heavens we call the vacuum of space ? Yeah, we do not find the sphere anywhere in it. In fact, very literally, it's the other way around. 

Space, including time, matter, and all we observe, lives in the sphere.

Every particle surface, every fluctuation, every arc by which light and other phenomena track against the supposed vacuum of space, all of it is a part of the same sphere. 

Let's say it again for emphasis, perhaps in a slightly different way, more condensed, even as we don't expect it to make sense quite yet, because this cannot be overstated:

Every part of the universe is in the sphere.

This may be difficult to square with our intuition about spheres, so we have a lot of work to do, undoubtedly, but for now suffice it to say that the extra dimensions and the spinning motion are crucial in a perhaps non-obvious way to transforming something that is normally simple and sorta round into something as vast and magnificent as the whole universe. We'll work on that.

Given its primacy in our conjecture, let's maybe capitalize The Sphere from now on, or perhaps refer to it as The Device, to disambiguate from the regular spheres we'll probably need to also talk about. 

Oh, and another point of clarity before we get way into it – there is no ambient space in which the Device lives. At least no ambient space of consequence. The Device itself is the limit of existence as we understand it, or, well, once we move off the device we have departed the realm of the explainable, even within the confines of our story that may be a delusion. Call the ambient space the great mystery, and note that it is irrelevant to our purpose of explaining how The Device gives rise to the universe. 

So The Sphere is just a sphere. This has a very precise mathematical meaning. 

A sphere is all points at a set distance – called its radius – from a center point.
This definition is very useful to us. It means that a point is not on the sphere unless it lives at precisely this set distance from the center (the distance is known as a radius). it's like a bubble, not a ball, but even calling it a bubble is generous because in truth it has zero thickness. not a small thickness, but none. all "nearby" points also live on the sphere, at the same exact radius.

ok, next reveal – the sphere has infinite dimensions.

before this reveal we may have been thinking about the ordinary bubbles we're used to. two dimensional "curved sheets" that exist in a three dimensional space. actually, let's revisit our intuition about dimensions – zero dimensions is a point, one dimension is a line, two dimensions is a plane, and three dimensions is a volume, remember ? very loosely speaking, of course. a poetic way of thinking about a dimension is that it's:

a new direction that casts no shadow

consider a point not on the established dimensional frame. now position a "light source" such that the point casts no shadow on the frame. or, well, a perfect little zero dimensional dot. the line between the light source and the point aligns perfectly with a new dimension.

 and this process can be repeated beyond three times, even if it is difficult to envision. math doesn't care.
